My name is Alex Tarchini, I am a qualified EN teacher and I have experience of teaching EN to various groups: starting from the youngest, to high school students, to university students and elderly. I have hold a MA in EN philology and teaching EN has always been what I really want to do.

I also have MA in BUS and POS, and I worked as a contract agent for 7 years in the EU Commission.

I am married and I have a cute little 2-year old son. I am Bulgarian, and I am very motivated, determined, and outgoing. Do you have any more questions about me...

II. Teaching Experience

1. What is your EN teaching experience:

- 1. Ethos (2008-2209): I have a MA degree in EN philology and have a professional qualification for an EN teacher.

- My experience: I first started teaching EN to children from disadvantaged groups as part of the social outreach programme of Ethos Open Hands Foundation. I was a volunteeer EN teacher to a group of about 10 kids. The course was 5 months, once per week at weekends. We initially started with high absenteesm, but bu the end of the course the kids finished all successfully.

- considering the diverse backgrounds and levels of the kids I designed my own programme as to adjust the programme to the needs of the kids and make the course more rleevant for them. E.g. Presentations about travel, cooking, study opportunites abroad. - For the grammar I was following an existing programme from textbook.

2. Teaching practice Sayanova (spring 2021): I was teaching EN to 3 groups of secondary and high school students. I was following a structured programme, but I did my best to adapt to the needs of the groups and to make it more relevant. 3 groups (8th, 9, 10th grades). 2 classes per day, every day for 2 months.

3. Private lessons at home to students of different ages: Mario (elderly), 2 other younger kids (6-8 years old). Taylored, TOEFL and SAT1 unviersity students.

4. Teaching practice (SWU, university): I would give also lessons in the regional capital as part of my teaching practice. Enjoyable experience to teach young people.

III. What makes you special: My background: I am a Roma qho grew up in the so called ghetto. Ever sicne I was little, my dream was to be a teacher and change the world around me.

11. Describe a particular problem that you encountered at work and how you went about solving:

C: I was teaching EN to kids from disadvantaged background. Very often they would be late or skip classes, which was very disruptive for the programme

A: To motivate the students to come on time I was making the lectures more interesting and attractive to them. In particular, I was asking more personal questions in EN (e.g. What they want to do in their life, and I would assign presentations on topics of their interest, so they were more involved). I was using visuals to attract their attention.

R: The attendance rate got better, and even more kids joined.
